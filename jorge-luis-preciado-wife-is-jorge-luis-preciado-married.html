    Biography of Jorge Luis Preciado

    Jorge Luis Preciado Rodríguez was born on August 9, 1972, in Coquimatlán, Colima. He is a distinguished Mexican politician affiliated with the National Action Party (Partido Acción Nacional, PAN). Preciado has held various significant roles in local and federal governance, demonstrating his commitment to public service and political leadership.

    Jorge Luis Preciado Rodríguez obtained a degree in Law from the University of Colima. His educational background also includes studies as a Technical Analyst in Programming, as well as Diplomas in Electoral Marketing and Electoral Campaigns from the University La Salle.

    Jorge Luis Preciado began his political journey as a member of the Institutional Revolutionary Party (Partido Revolucionario Institucional, PRI), where he served as the Leader of the Youth Revolutionary Front in his home state. However, since 1996, he has been an active member of the National Action Party.

    Within the PAN, Preciado has held various significant positions, including Secretary of Civic Education, Secretary of Training and Capacity Building, Campaign Coordinator, State and National Councilor, and President of the PAN in Colima.

During his time in the LIX Legislature, he was a member of committees such as Constitutional Points, Oversight of the Federal Audit Office (ASF), and the Committee for the Center of Legal Studies and Parliamentary Research. Additionally, he held the role of Secretary of the Committee on Regulations and Parliamentary Practices.

    In the 2012 Federal Elections of Mexico, Preciado secured a senatorial position as a First Minority Senator, with his formula receiving 34.65% of the votes, trailing behind the 46.72% obtained by the PRI.

    As a member of the LXII Legislature, Preciado actively participated in the Commissions on Communications and Transportation, Rural Development, and the Second Working Commission, focusing on Foreign Relations, National Defense, and Public Education. He also held positions as Secretary of the Commissions on Migration Affairs and Radio, Television, and Film. Furthermore, he served as the President of the Committees on Regulations and Parliamentary Practices, Migration Affairs, and the First Working Commission, concentrating on Governance, Constitutional Points, and Justice. Preciado also contributed to the Directing Committee of the Belisario Domínguez Institute.

    On May 21, 2013, Gustavo Madero, the President of the PAN, appointed Preciado as the President of the PAN Parliamentary Group in the Senate.

    On February 11, 2015, Preciado announced his intention to seek the candidacy for Governor of Colima under the PAN. Although he narrowly lost to the PRI candidate Ignacio Peralta by just 511 votes, Preciado’s gubernatorial campaign marked one of the closest races for the PAN in Colima. The election results were contested and eventually annulled, leading to a complex legal process involving allegations of document forgery by the PAN.
