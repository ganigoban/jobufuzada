    Princess Beatrice is paying tribute to her family through fashion.

    A recent photo taken during a London shopping trip shows the royal mom wearing three initials on a gold chain necklace: E for husband Edoardo Mapelli Mozzi, S for three-month-old baby daughter Sienna Elizabeth, and W for six-year-old stepson Christopher Woolf (who goes by the nickname Wolfie).

    Beatrice stayed warm in a fur-lined hooded cream jacket, a white sweater, a black skirt, black tights, and a pair of buckled black boots. Other accessories included a bright-pink floral face mask and a black knit hat with a brown pom-pom.

    BACKGRID

    The couple welcomed the arrival of little Sienna in late September and soon after confirmed her name in a statement.

    "We are delighted to share that we have named our daughter Sienna Elizabeth Mapelli Mozzi," Beatrice announced in a tweet at the time, which also included a photo of a footprint of her newborn's feet. "We are all doing well and Wolfie is the best big brother to Sienna."

    Beatrice joins Catherine, Duchess of Cambridge, and Meghan, Duchess of Sussex, in honoring her kids through jewelry. Duchess Kate has been photographed on multiple occasions wearing a custom gold Daniella Draper necklace, which retails at approximately $1,330 and is engraved with the initials of her three children, Prince George, Princess Charlotte, and Prince Louis.

    Duchess Meghan has also worn family-inspired accoutrements. In a video message posted on her 40th birthday, she accessorized with two necklaces designed by L.A.-based jewelry designer Logan Hollowell, featuring a Taurus constellation necklace for two-year-old son Archie's star sign and a Gemini necklace for seven-month-old baby girl Lilibet.
