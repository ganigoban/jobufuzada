    Magnus Carlsen is a Norwegian chess player and grandmaster who has a net worth of US$25 million, according to Celebrity Net Worth. He is reigning five-time World Chess Champion, who also holds the record for the longest unbeaten streak at the top level of classical chess. His primary source of income stems from playing chess tournaments and lucrative brand endorsement deals.

    Who is Magnus Carlsen?

    On the 30th day of November, 1990, Magnus Carlsen was born in Tnsberg, Norway to a chemical engineer Sigrun En and IT consultant Henrik Albert Carlsen. His family moved and lived for a short while in Espoo, Finland. They returned to Norway in 1998, settling at Lommedalen, Baerum before eventually making their way to Haslum.

    Carlsen had an early affinity for academic difficulties but was doing a 50-piece jigsaw puzzles at age four. He also enjoyed assembling Lego sets designed for older age groups, precisely 10 to 14 years.

    Carlsen’s father was an avid and amateur chess player, who started teaching him when he was 5 years old , although he showed lack of enthusiasm. He has three siblings, all sisters, He revealed that one motivating factor to take up chess was when he beat his oldest sister in a chess game.

    Carlsen went to the Norwegian College of Elite Sport, where he was coached by the nation’s top player, Grandmaster Simen Agdestein. From 2000 through 2002, Carlsen had played in nearly 300 rated tournament games, in addition to several blitz tournaments and minor events. Toward the end of the latter year, he came in sixth place in the European Under-12 Championship, and then tied for first place in the World Under-12 Championship.
