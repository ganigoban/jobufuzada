    Who is Kevin Youkilis?

    Kevin Youkilis was born on March 15, 1979, in Cincinnati, Ohio, to a Jewish family. He attended Sycamore High School and played a variety of positions on the Aviators baseball team.

    They won the Amateur Athletic Union National Championship in 1994, and Kevin was a four-year letterman, two-time All-Greater Miami Conference and All-City selection, and All-State selection his senior year when he led the team with a.475 batting average and finished second all-time in home runs. Youkili was named into the Sycamore Athletic Hall of Fame in 2008, and the school retired his number 13 in 2017.

    Kevin Youkilis remained close to home, playing college baseball for the Cincinnati Bearcats. He became noted for his ability to get on base and scrappiness as a player, and he flourished despite many people doubting his athleticism. His instructors referred to him as “roly-poly,” “pudgy,” and “fat kid” frequently.

    In 2007, Youkilis was inducted into the University of Cincinnati’s James P. Kelly Athletics Hall of Fame, and his No. 36 jersey was retired in 2015. Youkilis earned a bus iness degree from the University of Cincinnati and was named to the Conference USA Hall of Fame in its inaugural class.

    What is Kevin Youkilis’s career?

    The Boston Red Sox selected Kevin Youkilis in the eighth round (243rd overall) of the 2001 Major League Baseball draft. He made his professional debut in the minor leagues, playing third base, but gradually transitioned to first base early in his career. On May 15, 2004, he made his Major League Baseball debut with the Red Sox, making the World Series roster but not playing in the team’s championship win.

    Kevin Youkilis won the Gold Glove Award in 2007 for his contributions to the Boston Red Sox’s victory in the World Series. Youkilis was a three-time All-Star (2008, 2009, 2011), and he won the AL Hank Aaron Award in 2008.

    The right-hander formerly played for the Chicago White Sox in 2012, the New York Yankees in 2013, and Japan’s Tohoku Rakuten Golden Eagles in the Nippon Professional Baseball Pacific League in 2014.

    He retired from Major League Baseball on October 30, 2014. He last played for the Yankees on June 13, 2013. Kevin Youkilis had a.281 batting average in his MLB career, with 150 home runs and 618 RBIs.

    In January 2009, Kevin Youkilis signed a four-year, $41.1 million contract with the Boston Red Sox. In 2012, he signed a one-year agreement with the New York Yankees worth $12 million. Kevin Youkilis made approximately $52 million in pay alone throughout his nine seasons in the Major Leagues.
